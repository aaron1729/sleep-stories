1. The Eiffel Tower, Paris
- It stands at 324 meters, making it the tallest structure in Paris.
- Constructed from 1887 to 1889 as the entrance to the 1889 World's Fair.
- Named after the engineer Gustave Eiffel, whose company designed and built the tower.
- It is often seen as a symbol of romance and has appeared in countless films and books set in Paris.
- Enjoy crepes or baguettes from a local vendor nearby the Eiffel Tower.
- On a riverboat cruise along the Seine, observe as the Tower’s lights twinkle in the darkness, accompanied by the laughter and clinking of glasses as everyone marvels at the sight.

2. The Colosseum, Rome
- An elliptical amphitheatre in the centre of the city, it is considered one of the greatest works of Roman architecture and Roman engineering.
- Capable of seating 50,000 spectators, the Colosseum was used for gladiatorial contests, public spectacles, and dramas based on Classical mythology.
- The Colosseum was built by the Emperor Vespasian in 72 AD, and was completed by his son Titus in 80 AD.
- Indulge in authentic Italian gelato from a nearby gelateria while strolling around the Colosseum.
- From a riverboat on the Tiber, passengers can glimpse the Colosseum, imagining the sounds of the ancient games carried by the wind.

3. The Statue of Liberty, New York
- A gift from the people of France to the United States, it was designed by French sculptor Frédéric Auguste Bartholdi and built by Gustave Eiffel.
- The statue was dedicated on October 28, 1886, and has since become a global symbol of freedom and democracy.
- The famous sonnet "The New Colossus" by Emma Lazarus is inscribed on its pedestal, which includes the iconic quote "Give me your tired, your poor, Your huddled masses yearning to breathe free".
- Try a classic New York hot dog from a vendor near Battery Park, where ferry departures to the Statue can be found.
- On a riverboat cruise around the New York Harbor, get up close and personal with the Statue of Liberty, feeling the breeze in your hair as you snap photos and listen to the excited chatter of those around you.
