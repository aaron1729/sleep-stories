1. Eiffel Tower
- This iconic structure was built in 1889 as the entrance arch for the World's Fair.
- It is named after the engineer Gustave Eiffel, whose company designed and built the tower.
- Ernest Hemingway once said: "It is by riding a bicycle that you learn the contours of a country best, since you have to sweat up the hills and coast down them. You remember them as they actually are, while in a motor car only a high hill impresses you, and you have no such accurate remembrance of the country you have driven through as you gain by riding a bicycle." This quote resonates especially when viewing the Eiffel tower from a leisurely river cruise.
- Experience: Enjoy the view of this magnificent structure as you depart on your river cruise, sipping on a glass of fine French wine.

*****
2. Notre-Dame Cathedral
- The Cathedral started to be built in 1163 and is famous for its French Gothic architecture.
- Victor Hugo's novel "The Hunchback of Notre-Dame" takes place in the cathedral.
- After being damaged in a fire in 2019, Notre-Dame stirs emotions of love, loss, and hope in Parisians and tourists alike.
- Experience: As you approach the cathedral, take a moment to consult your map and locate other historical buildings in its vicinity.

*****
3. Le Marais
- Once the city's Jewish quarter, Le Marais today is filled with hip boutiques, galleries, and cafés.
- It hosts the Museum of Jewish Art and History, chronicling the history of Jews in France since the Middle Ages.
- Quote by composer Erik Satie, who used to live in Le Marais: "I took to my room and let small things evolve slowly.”
- Dining: Try the famous falafel at one of the many eateries in the district.
- Experience: Take in the beautiful architecture of the half-timbered houses, a sight not often seen in Paris.

*****
4. Louvre Museum
- The Louvre was originally a fortress built in the late 12th century, now the world's largest art museum.
- Leonardo da Vinci's "Mona Lisa" is among its many treasures.
- The museum features heavily in the popular novel and movie "The Da Vinci Code."
- Experience: From the boat, marvel at the stunning sight of the Louvre pyramid against the Parisian sky.

*****
5. Tuileries Garden
- The garden, created by Catherine de Medici in 1564, was the first public park in Paris.
- In Marcel Proust's novel "In Search of Lost Time", one of the characters, Swann, takes a walk in the Garden.
- Dining: Enjoy a traditional Parisian picnic in these gardens. Baguette, cheese, and wine are must-haves.
- Experience: Notice the beauty of the various sculptures and the vibrant flowers blooming in the Tuileries Garden as your boat gently cruises by.

*****
6. Place de la Concorde
- At the east end of the Champs-Élysées, it is the largest public square in Paris.
- During the French Revolution, it was named 'Place de la Révolution', and a guillotine was installed in the center.
- Dining: Nearby cafes are renowned for their delectable croissants.
- Experience: Look out for the Luxor Obelisk, a 3,300-year-old Egyptian obelisk erected on the square.

*****
7. Musée d'Orsay
- Housed in the former Gare d'Orsay, a Beaux-Arts railway station.
- Predominantly features French art, including works of Impressionists like Monet and Renoir.
- The museum appeared in the Woody Allen's movie "Midnight in Paris."
- Experience: As the boat cruises by, marvel at the grand clock face on the museum's exterior.

*****
8. Les Invalides
- A complex of buildings containing museums and monuments, all relating to military history of France.
- Houses the Dôme des Invalides, a large church with the burial site for some of France's war heroes, notably Napoleon Bonaparte.
- Experience: Feel the breeze as the boat glides past this complex, and admire the golden dome that stands out against the blue sky.

*****
9. Champs-Élysées
- Known as "The most beautiful avenue in the world."
- Named after the Greek paradise for heroic spirits, "Elysium."
- Shopping and dining: Many high-end boutiques and cafes line this avenue.
- Experience: Captivate yourself with the excitement and grandeur of this avenue from the comfort of your riverboat.

*****
10. Pont Alexandre III
- Hailed as the most extravagant bridge in Paris.
- Built in Art Nouveau style for the 1900 Exposition Universelle (world's fair).
- Named after Tsar Alexander III, who had concluded the Franco-Russian Alliance in 1892.
- Experience: As your cruise passes under the bridge, look up to admire its detailed sculptures and ornate lamps.

*****
11. Grand Palais
- Built for the Paris Exhibition of 1900, it hosts various events and exhibitions.
- The Grand Palais is noted for its large glass dome, mirroring the trend of using glass and iron in architecture during the Industrial Revolution.
- Dining: The Mini Palais restaurant inside the Grand Palais offers fresh seasonal produce.
- Experience: Snap a few pictures of the Grand Palais's stunning exterior from the river, sunlight glinting off its immense glass dome.
