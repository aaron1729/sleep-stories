1. The Statue of Liberty
- Gifted by France to the United States in the late 1800s, it's a symbol of freedom and democracy.
- The inscription on its base includes the famous quote from Emma Lazarus' poem: "Give me your tired, your poor, Your huddled masses yearning to breathe free".
- You may recognize it from the final scene in the film "Planet of the Apes".
- By ferry, you can enjoy the stunning view of Manhattan skyline and capture it with your camera.
*****
2. Central Park
- It's an urban park in Manhattan established in 1857 and extended to its current size in 1873.
- Literature fans may recognize it from J.D. Salinger's iconic novel "Catcher in the Rye".
- Central Park is the most visited urban park in the United States.
- While wandering, you may stumble across street performers or musicians sharing their talents.
*****
3. Times Square
- Named after the New York Times which moved its headquarters there in 1904.
- The New Year’s Eve Ball drop atop One Times Square started in 1907 and continues till date.
- Judy Garland's film "Meet Me in St. Louis" features a song about the famous square.
- Street food vendors selling hot dogs and pretzels are a common sight here. 
*****
4. Empire State Building
- Construction was completed in 1931, and it was the tallest building in the world until 1971.
- Known from literature in Tom Wolfe's novel "The Bonfire of the Vanities".
- Famous quote: "It's the closest thing to heaven in this city" from "An Affair to Remember".
- Notice the incredible architecture details as you take the elevator to the observatory.
*****
5. Broadway Theater District
- Home to 41 professional theaters, most of which can accommodate over 500 people.
- It's the setting of "The Phantom of the Opera," the longest-running show in Broadway history.
- "The neon lights are bright on Broadway" is a lyric from "On Broadway" song by The Drifters.
- The lottery system for getting show tickets can be exciting for visitors.
*****
6. Metropolitan Museum of Art
- It’s the largest museum in the US, founded in 1870.
- The Met features in the popular Young Adult novel "From the Mixed-Up Files of Mrs. Basil E. Frankweiler".
- After browsing the exhibits, sit on the museum steps and people-watch, just like in the TV show "Gossip Girl".
*****
7. The High Line
- A former elevated railway track converted into a public park.
- Features prominently in several novels, including Jennifer Egan's "A Visit from the Goon Squad".
- Grab an ice cream from a nearby vendor and sit on one of the loungers as trains pass beneath you.
*****
8. One World Observatory
- Atop the tallest building in the Western Hemisphere, you'll find this stunning observatory.
- It's constructed on the site where the Twin Towers once stood.
- A quote from the welcome message, "See forever," has become a slogan for the observatory.
- The interactive displays in the elevator retelling NYC's history are captivating.
*****
9. Brooklyn Bridge
- Completed in 1883, it's one of the oldest roadway bridges in the United States.
- Hart Crane's epic poem "The Bridge" was inspired by the Brooklyn bridge.
- After crossing the bridge, enjoy pizza from Grimaldi's, a famous pizzeria in Dumbo area of Brooklyn.
- Walking across the bridge, enjoy the views of the Manhattan skyline and the East River.
*****
10. Grand Central Terminal
- The beautiful Beaux-Arts building was completed in 1913.
- The terminal features in the opening scene of the novel "The Catcher in the Rye"
- Vanderbilt Hall, once a waiting room, is now used for the Market, public events, and exhibitions.
- Discover the Whispering Gallery and share secret messages with friends across the room.
