Colosseum, Rome: An iconic architectural wonder known as the world's largest amphitheater where gladiatorial contests were hosted in the past.

- Colosseum was originally known as the Flavian Amphitheater and was commissioned around A.D. 70-72 by Emperor Vespasian.
- It could hold an estimated 50,000 to 80,000 spectators at maximum capacity.
- It was also used for other public events such as animal hunts, executions, re-enactments of famous battles, and dramas based on Classical mythology.
- Beneath the Colosseum, a complex series of tunnels known as the Hypogeum were used to house animals and slaves.
- "While stands the Colosseum, Rome shall stand; When falls the Colosseum, Rome shall fall; And when Rome fallsâ€”the World." This quote by Lord Byron illustrates the importance of the Colosseum in literature and arts.
- A typical dining experience around the Colosseum is best characterized by classic Roman dishes like Carbonara or Amatriciana, enjoyed at a nearby Trattoria.
- Navigating the narrow and busy Roman streets with your Vespa scooter on your way to the Colosseum is a thrilling experience. Parking up, you can take a moment to feel the warm Italian sun on your skin and marvel at the awe-inspiring history that stands tall before you. Local guides are often seen around, making you feel welcomed with their warm smiles and offers of historical insight into the majestic structure.

Venetian Market, Venice: A bustling outdoor market packed with fresh seafood, fruits, and traditional Venetian goods that offers an immersive local culture experience.

- The market dates back to the 11th century, serving as the vibrant and colorful heartbeat of the Venetian culinary scene.
- Located near the Rialto Bridge, this was the economic center of Venice in the Middle Ages.
- Italian poet Petrarch referenced Venice's markets in his writings, describing the city as "rich in merchandise and men from every shore".
- Visitors can savor the experience of tasting cicchetti, a traditional Venetian bar snack, which often involves fresh seafood from the market.
- Riding a Vespa to the market, you can weave through the narrow cobblestone streets, feeling the cool morning air and smell of fresh, salty seafood as you approach the bustling market.
