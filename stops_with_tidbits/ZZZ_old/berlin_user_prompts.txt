Berlin Television Tower (Fernsehturm)
At 368 meters, it's the tallest structure in Germany.
The tower's sphere features a revolving restaurant.
Constructed between 1965 and 1969 by the government of the German Democratic Republic (GDR).
*****
East Side Gallery
It's the longest remaining part of the Berlin Wall.
Features over 100 murals by artists from all over the world.
The most famous painting is the "Fraternal Kiss" between Brezhnev and Honecker.
*****
Brandenburg Gate
Built in the 18th century as a symbol of peace.
Napoleon once marched through the gate’s arches to take the city.
Ronald Reagan famously said, "Mr. Gorbachev, tear down this wall!" near this site.
*****
Reichstag Building
Burned down in 1933 in an event that marked the rise of the Nazi regime.
Norman Foster led the reconstruction after reunification, adding the glass dome.
The inscription "Dem Deutschen Volke" (To the German people) adorns the façade.
*****
Berlin Zoological Gardens
Home to Knut, the famous polar bear cub raised by zookeepers.
Houses over 20,000 animals from almost 1,400 species.
Visitors often enjoy a "Brezel" (pretzel) while strolling through the zoo.
*****
Checkpoint Charlie
The name comes from the NATO phonetic alphabet (Alpha, Bravo, Charlie).
Scene of tank standoff between the US and Soviet forces in 1961.
Now hosts a replica of the original guardhouse and a checkpoint booth.
*****
Museum Island
Consists of five internationally significant museums.
Was designed as a "sanctuary of art and science" in the 19th century.
You can try "Königsberger Klopse," a traditional German dish, in nearby restaurants.
*****
Soviet Memorial in Treptower Park
It's one of the largest Soviet war memorials outside of the USSR.
The central statue is a Soviet soldier holding a German child.
The memorial is also the resting place for over 5,000 Soviet soldiers.
*****
Bebelplatz
Known for the Nazi book burning ceremony in 1933.
"That was only a prelude; where they burn books, they will ultimately burn people as well." – Heinrich Heine, whose quote predates the event by over a century.
Nearby, people often enjoy "Berliner Pfannkuchen," a traditional jam-filled doughnut.
*****
Victory Column
Commemorates the Prussian victory in the Danish-Prussian War.
After WWII, the column was moved from its original location in front of the Reichstag by the French.
Visitors might eat "Currywurst," a fast-food dish of steamed, then fried pork sausage cut into slices and seasoned with curry ketchup.
